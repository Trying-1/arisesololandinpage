Bible App – Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how the Bible App ("we", "our", or "us") handles your information when you use our mobile application and any related services made available through this website. As of the effective date below, the Bible App does not require you to create an account or sign in, and we do not collect or process any personal data that directly identifies you (such as your name, email address, or contact details).

Effective date: 1 Jan 2026 Applies to: Bible App (closed testing and production)

1. Overview

The Bible App is designed to provide Scripture reading and related spiritual resources. We have intentionally designed the current version of the app so that you can use it without creating an account and without sharing personal information with us. We do not sell, rent, or trade any personal information.

2. Information We Collect

As of now, the Bible App does not collect any personal data such as your name, email address, phone number, or other contact details. We also do not require registration, sign-in, or authentication.

  • Local on‑device data only. Any app settings (for example, dark mode, language selection, or last-read chapter) are stored locally on your device and are not transmitted to our servers.
  • No personal analytics profile. We do not build a personal analytics profile of you across sessions, and we do not attempt to identify you as an individual user.
  • Crash information (if enabled by your platform). Your device or app store platform (for example, Google Play) may provide anonymous, aggregated crash and performance information. This data is typically not directly identifiable to us and is used only to help improve stability.
  • No optional data collection features. The current version of the Bible App does not include optional analytics, advertising SDKs, or user tracking that collect additional personal information.

3. How We Use Your Information

Because we do not collect personal data within the app, there is very little information for us to process. In practice, we rely mainly on local storage on your device and, where provided by the app store, anonymous crash and performance reports. This is used solely to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the Bible App and its core features.
  • Store your in-app preferences, reading progress, and customisations locally on your device.
  • Monitor performance, fix bugs, and improve the stability and quality of the app in general.

4. Third-Party Services

The current version of the Bible App does not include third-party advertising SDKs or user tracking libraries. The app may rely on standard services provided by the app store platform (for example, Google Play) for installation, distribution, and basic, anonymised crash reporting.

Please also review the privacy policy of the app store or device provider you use (for example, Google or your device manufacturer), as they may independently collect information necessary to operate their services.

5. Data Retention

Because we do not maintain user accounts or collect personal data within the app, there is no central user database for us to retain. Any preferences or reading progress saved by the app are stored locally on your device and remain there until you clear the app data or uninstall the app from your device.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Since we do not operate user accounts or maintain a user database, most data management actions are under your direct control on your device. If you wish to remove data such as reading progress or preferences, you can do so by clearing the app data or uninstalling the app on your device.

If you have any questions about how the Bible App handles information, you can still contact us using the details in the Contact Us section below.

7. Children's Privacy

The Bible App is not directed to children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so that we can take appropriate action.

8. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organisational measures designed to protect your information against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or alteration. However, no method of electronic transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the Bible App, legal requirements, or best practices. When we make material changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, provide additional notice within the app.
